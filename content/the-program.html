Student Code of Conduct (SCC)

The SCC is not intended to address the entire spectrum of student misbehavior that may occur during the program. Some examples of inappropriate behaviors are

  • Displaying any behavior that is disruptive to the orderly process of classroom instruction.
  • Initiating or participating in any unacceptable physical actions that include fighting / bullying.
  • Disobeying the authority of the Instructor.
  • Clothing: The student is expected to wear proper Islamic clothing (preferably thobe, shalwar kamis, topi). This does not include shorts, sports cap, tank top and clothing with faces, characters, indecent slogans or pictures.
  • Supplies: Students must bring a pen/pencil,notebook and folder every day to class. Loose leaf paper does not count as a notebook.
  • Cell Phones and other devices: Cell phones, ipods and any other devices must be switched off during class times. If these have to be on for any emergency reason, the student must get the Instructorsâ€™s permission.
  • The Instructor will decide on the action to be taken to address all student misconduct. Under extreme circumstances, this may include expelling the student.
